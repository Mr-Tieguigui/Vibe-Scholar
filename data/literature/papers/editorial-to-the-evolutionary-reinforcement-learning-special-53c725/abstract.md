# Editorial to the “Evolutionary Reinforcement Learning” Special Issue

Evolutionary Reinforcement Learning combines the strengths of evolutionary algorithms with reinforcement learning techniques. This special issue offers readers a diverse range of contributions that illuminate different aspects of this hybrid approach. In “Combining Evolution and Deep Reinforcement Learning for Policy Search: A Survey,” Olivier Sigaud presents a comprehensive review of the burgeoning field that merges evolution and deep reinforcement learning. A systematic categorization of 45 algorithms published after 2017 provides a framework for understanding the sprawling diversity of approaches based on the mechanistic integration of the two techniques and hints at the potential for designing new hybrid mechanisms. In “P2P Energy Trading through Prospect Theory, Differential Evolution, and Reinforcement Learning,” Ashutosh Timilsina and Simone Silvestri present a decentralized energy-trading framework that integrates behavioral economics, reinforcement learning, and differential evolution— demonstrating the usefulness of this hybrid approach on a practical problem. They tackle computational complexity with their Differential Evolution-based Algorithm for Trading Energy and introduce two reinforcement learning pricing mechanisms, achieving marked improvements in both buyers’ and sellers’ outcomes when validated with real-world data, creating more efficient and personalized peer-to-peer energy markets. In “Curiosity Creates Diversity in Policy Search” by Paul-Antoine Le Tolguenec, Emmanuel Rachelson, Yann Besse, and Dennis G. Wilson, the authors delve into the challenge of optimal policy exploration in reward-sparse environments. They introduce Curiosity-ES, an evolutionary strategy that employs Curiosity as an intrinsic fitness metric to guide policy search. The article showcases how Curiosity-ES surpasses existing Quality-Diversity methods like MAP-Elites in tasks such as maze navigation and robotic control, and it posits that Curiosity encourages a natural diversity in policy making by emphasizing unexplored or under-explored transitions. This issue’s contributions reveal the expansive scope and dynamic potential of Evolutionary Reinforcement Learning, and they invite readers to further engage with these pioneering works to uncover their broader implications and applications.
