# Getting Started with VibeScholar

## Welcome

Welcome to **VibeScholar** — an opinionated research-operations platform that helps you plan, execute, and track AI/ML research projects from idea to publication.

This project is your interactive tutorial. Each section below explains a core VibeScholar concept. Follow the steps in the **Vibe Coding** and **Vibe Research** panels on the left to learn by doing.

## What is VibeScholar?

VibeScholar organizes every research project into five pillars:

1. **Overview** — Structured project definition with motivation, scope, questions, and risks
2. **Execution** — Phased Vibe Coding (VC) and Vibe Research (VR) steps with prompts and acceptance criteria
3. **Timeline** — Milestone-based tracking with Kanban view and deadline management
4. **Artifacts** — Registry of code, datasets, figures, tables, and reports linked to milestones
5. **Literature** — Imported papers with tags, summaries, and normalized metadata

## Quick Tour

### Step 1 — Explore the Dashboard
Open the project list to see all 62 demo projects organized across 16 research domains. Click any project card to see its overview, progress bars, and RAG status.

### Step 2 — Open the Gold Standard Project
Navigate to **Tool-Budget Robustness Benchmark for Agentic Research Systems**. This is the most detailed demo project with:
- 11 structured overview sections
- 12 Vibe Coding steps with rich prompts across 6 phases
- 10 Vibe Research steps with detailed writing guidance
- 20 artifacts linked to milestones
- 8 milestones with acceptance criteria
- 12 literature references with tags

### Step 3 — Try the Execution Panel
Open any project's VC or VR steps. Each step has:
- **Title & Description** — What to do
- **Acceptance Criteria** — How to know you are done
- **Prompt Hint** — A one-line prompt you can paste into an AI assistant
- **Prompt Expanded** — A detailed, multi-paragraph prompt with context, plan, and verification steps
- **Deliverables** — Expected outputs for this step
- **Status** — Track progress (todo → doing → done)

### Step 4 — Check the Timeline
Open the milestone panel to see deadline-driven tracking. Milestones link to execution steps and artifacts. Use the Kanban view to drag milestones between status columns.

### Step 5 — Browse Literature
The literature panel shows imported papers with tags. You can filter by tag, search by title, and view summaries. Import new papers via CSV or JSONL.

## Key Concepts

- **Vibe Coding (VC):** Implementation-focused steps — code, tests, infrastructure, packaging
- **Vibe Research (VR):** Research-focused steps — literature review, experimental design, writing, figures
- **Phase Grouping:** Steps are organized into 6 phases (0–5) from setup to finalization
- **DesignSpec:** A structured project specification that auto-generates execution steps, milestones, and artifacts
- **RAG Status:** Red/Amber/Green health indicator per project
